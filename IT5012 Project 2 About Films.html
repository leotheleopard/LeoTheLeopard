The Films We Screen

The Shawshank Redemption (1994)

Two imprisoned men bond over a number of years, finding solace and eventual redemption through acts of common decency.

Directed By: Frank Darabont


The Godfather (1972)

The aging patriarch of an organized crime dynasty transfers control of his clandestine empire to his reluctant son.

Directed By: Francis Ford Coppola


The Godfather: Part II (1974)

The early life and career of Vito Corleone in 1920s New York City is portrayed, while his son, Michael, expands and tightens his grip on the family crime syndicate.

Directed By: Francis Ford Coppola


The Dark Knight (2008)

When The Joker wreaks havoc and chaos on the people of Gotham, Batman must accept one of the greatest psychological and physical tests of his ability to fight injustice.

Directed By: Christopher Nolan


12 Angry Men (1957)

A jury holdout attempts to prevent a miscarriage of justice by forcing his colleagues to reconsider the evidence.

Directed By: Sidney Lumet


Schindler's List

In Poland during World War II, Oskar Schindler becomes concerned for his Jewish workforce after witnessing their persecution.

Directed By: Steven Spielberg


The Lord of the Rings: Return of the King (2003)

Gandalf and Aragorn lead the World of Men against Sauron to distract him from Frodo and Sam as they approach Mount Doom.

Directed By: Peter Jackson


Pulp Fiction (1994)

The lives of two mob hitmen, a boxer, a gangster and his wife, and a pair of diner bandits intertwine in four tales of violence and redemption.

Directed By: Quentin Tarantino


The Good, the Bad and the Ugly (1966)

A bounty hunting scam joins two men in an uneasy alliance against a third in a race to find a fortune in gold buried in a remote cemetery.

Directed By: Sergio Leone


The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring (2001)

A Hobbit from the Shire and eight companions set out on a journey to destroy the powerful One Ring and save Middle-earth.

Directed By: Peter Jackson
